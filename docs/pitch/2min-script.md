# Heart of Virtue — 2-Minute Spoken Script

*Tone: calm, unhurried, like telling someone about a book that changed you.*

---

Here's the setup.

A man named Jean Claire is in a coma in a hospital somewhere. His wife and daughter were killed — a bombing, while they were on a Catholic pilgrimage. Jean survived. And the grief was too much.

So his mind built him a world.

He wakes up in Aurelion — a medieval place, stone and mist — believing he's a crusader on a holy mission. He doesn't remember his wife. He doesn't remember his daughter. His subconscious sealed that away.

That's where you start.

Heart of Virtue is a text-based RPG — browser playable, no download — and it follows Jean through this world as the memories start to crack through. You're solving the problems of Aurelion: you're fighting creatures in a turn-based tactical combat system, forming relationships with companions, exploring a world that was — and this is the important part — *built specifically for Jean* to help him grieve and choose.

The question at the center of the game is one of the oldest questions people ask: "Why did God let this happen?" And here's the honest thing about this game — it doesn't answer that question. Because real life doesn't either. What it does is follow Jean to the moment where he can choose to keep going anyway, not because he has answers, but because choosing matters even without them.

If you've played Planescape: Torment, or if you've read C.S. Lewis after a loss — this is in that territory.

Chapters 1 and 2 are playable right now, free, in your browser. About one to two hours of content, and a story that will stay with you.

nexusfidei.dev/games/HeartOfVirtue.

Come find out who Jean Claire is.
