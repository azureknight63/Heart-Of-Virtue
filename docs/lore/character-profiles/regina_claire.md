# Regina Claire
## Overview
- Name: Regina Claire
- Age: early childhood in Jean's memories (expressed as a cherished echo)
- Occupation: Child of a small family in Jean's remembered life; in Aurelion she appears as a fragile, bright memory and a beacon for Jean's protective instincts.
- Appearance: A small, curious child with light hair often tangled from play and wide, questioning eyes. In recollection she wears a patched dress and clutches a hastily drawn picture or a bit of ribbon.
- Personality: Curious, resilient, and stubborn in small, meaningful ways. Regina is quick to laugh, slow to anger, and carries a quiet wisdom beyond her years — the kind children have when they accept both wonder and fear as part of the day.
- Background: Regina is the daughter of Jean and Amelia. Her earliest memories are formed by bedtime stories, shared chores that became games, and the safety of a household where small rituals mattered. Her presence in Jean's memory is both tender and painful; she represents what was protected and what he failed to fully shield.

## Important relationships
- Jean Claire: Father. Regina is Jean's living reminder of his promises and the motivation for many of his choices in Aurelion.
- Amelia Claire: Mother (deceased). Regina's relationship with Amelia is the softest part of Jean's grief — the lullabies, the scraped-knee kisses, and the quiet lessons about kindness.
- Gorran: Strange and foreign to Regina's original life, but in Jean's mind Gorran becomes a symbol of the strange guardianship Jean imagines he must provide to keep Regina safe in a fractured world.

## Role in the Story
Regina functions as both motivation and moral compass. Her vulnerability urges Jean toward protection and restitution; her small acts of independence and compassion remind him what ordinary courage looks like. Regina's memories can trigger quests to recover or recreate elements of home — a drawing, a toy, a lost rhyme — and can also surface moral dilemmas where Jean must choose between safety and honesty.

Regina's scenes are often quiet but emotionally charged: a drawing pinned to a wall, a half-remembered lullaby, or the echo of a child's laugh that guides Jean to a clue. Her presence can also be used to reveal Jean's failures and his capacity to atone.

## Themes and Motifs
- Childhood and Innocence: Regina embodies small, everyday wonder tempered by the fragility of a disturbed world.
- Hope and Continuity: She represents the future Jean wants to protect and the small continuity that persists across loss.
- Lessons and Small Courage: Regina shows that bravery can be a scraped knee, a shared snack, or a whispered night's prayer.

## Suggested In-Game Touches
- Items: A crayon-scribbled drawing that unlocks a memory scene; a small copper coin Regina carried that can be used as a key or talisman; a worn ribbon that grants a brief protective aura when used.
- Encounters: Dream glimpses where Regina offers a childlike clue that adults miss; side-quests to restore a small domestic object that calms Jean and unlocks dialog options.
