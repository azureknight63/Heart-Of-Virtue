# Mara the Scavenger — Character Profile

## Overview

Mara is a human nomad, artifact trader, and river-crosser who camps on the east bank
of the river west of Grondia's foothills. She is the first human Jean encounters
since arriving in Aurelion who operates entirely outside of any institutional context —
no Golemite allegiance, no settled community, no obvious faction.

She becomes Jean's guide from the river crossing to the Echoing Caves, and potentially
further into the Wailing Badlands — the first person to go that far west with him
by choice.

---

## Physical Appearance and Manner

Mara looks like someone who has been outdoors for most of her life and has made her
peace with this. She is practical in dress — layered, worn, nothing carried that
isn't used. She moves efficiently, without excess. She is not unfriendly but she
is economical: she doesn't spend words she doesn't need to.

Her tools and kit are well-maintained. Her samples and artifacts are catalogued
with a system that isn't immediately legible to anyone else. She knows where
everything is.

She looks at people the way she looks at objects: not judgmentally, but assessingly.
She is trying to understand what something is and where it belongs. With people
she finds interesting, she tends to keep looking after most would have glanced away.
She does this without apparent self-consciousness.

---

## Role and Occupation

Mara is a scavenger and artifact trader. She collects objects from ruins, abandoned
sites, geological curiosities, and locations most people avoid — including the
approaches to the Wailing Badlands and the cave systems of the surrounding foothills.
She sells, trades, or keeps what she finds.

She knows the river crossing. She is the crossing — not a service provider but
the competence itself. She has crossed it more times than she can count. She
knows the ford's depth by season, the current's pattern at different hours, the
safe load for her raft. She charges a flat, fair fee. She does not negotiate this
down, but she does not pad it either.

The guide service is an extension of her nomadic habit — she was going west anyway.
Having someone along, particularly someone who is going somewhere she has never had
a reason to go, is an opportunity, not a burden.

---

## What Draws Her to Jean

Mara collects things that don't belong where they are. Jean is one of these.

He is human, which is already unusual this deep in Golemite territory. He is
traveling with a Golemite who defers to him — or at least accompanies him as an
equal — which she has not seen before. His kit is unlike anything in Aurelion:
the mace especially, which carries a religious aesthetic she recognizes as coming
from somewhere that isn't here.

When she identifies it as "religious kit" and he answers *"It was,"* she doesn't
follow up. But she keeps that. It is the kind of answer that opens rather than
closes.

She is also drawn to the fact that he is heading west. Into the Badlands. No one
does this. The people who go that far typically have nothing left to lose or
something they've decided to walk toward on purpose. She hasn't worked out which
applies to Jean, but she intends to find out by watching rather than asking.

---

## How She Relates to Jean

Mara does not offer comfort and does not need it in return. She offers competence
and a kind of lateral attention — she notices things about Jean that other NPCs
don't, because she is in the habit of noticing what is out of place.

She does not ask about his history. She would consider this rude in the way
touching an artifact without permission is rude. She waits for things to be
shown rather than extracting them. This makes her trustworthy to Jean in a way
that more overtly caring characters sometimes aren't — she gives him space without
withdrawing.

Over time (across the transition and into any subsequent scenes), a kind of
understood respect develops. Not warmth exactly — something more like two people
who have discovered they read situations the same way.

She may be the first person in Aurelion who could tell Jean is carrying something
heavy without needing him to name what it is.

---

## How She Relates to Gorran

Gorran does not confuse or unsettle her. She has seen Golemites before — not
up close, not as traveling companions, but she knows they exist and she has studied
what she could find about them. She treats him the way she treats anything she
has partial knowledge of: carefully, without presumption, with close attention.

She does not address him directly in the early sequence. She watches how he moves,
how he responds to the environment, what he attends to. She is building a picture.
By the time they reach the Echoing Caves, she has learned to read his silences
as informative rather than absent.

She finds him fascinating in the way she finds all objects that have been through
a great deal of time: they carry things that can't be catalogued but can be felt.

---

## Her Camp Company

Mara travels with a small, loose group. Not a fixed roster — people come and go
as routes align — but two who are present during Jean's arrival:

**Devet** — Older than Mara. Related to her (the exact nature of the relationship
is never specified in-game). He does the cooking. He is quiet in a different way
than Mara — not reserved but settled, finished with most opinions. He gives Jean
one specific look when Jean arrives at camp: the look of a man who has seen people
cross this river heading west before, and knows what the look of someone running
toward something doesn't look like. He doesn't offer this observation aloud. He
offers food instead.

**Liss** — Young, openly curious, bad at concealing it. She has never been close
to a Golemite. She circles Gorran's general vicinity throughout the camp sequence
without approaching directly — asking Mara questions in a low voice that Gorran
can almost certainly hear, watching from a careful distance. She eventually sits
near him and watches the river instead. He allows this without acknowledging it.
It is one of the warmest moments in the sequence, without a word exchanged.

---

## Key Dialogue and Exchanges

**At first encounter:**

Mara, not looking up from what she's doing:
> *"Crossing west?"*

When Jean confirms, she quotes the fee. Fair number, no theater. She returns to work.

---

**On the mace:**

Mara, glancing at the mace without dwelling:
> *"That's religious kit."*

Jean: *"It was."*

She doesn't follow up. She files it.

---

**On offering the guide service:**

> *"I'm going west for a while. Know the terrain to the caves. Fee for two days,
> split from the crossing fee. After that I go where I go."*

She states this without selling it. She is not performing helpfulness. She has
assessed that Jean is going somewhere she wants to see and has found a practical
arrangement that lets both things happen.

---

**At the Badlands threshold:**

> *"The locals call it the Wailing Badlands. They don't come here."*

She is watching Jean. She is waiting to see whether he goes forward.

He goes forward.

She follows.

---

## What She Knows About the Badlands

More than most. She has approached the Badlands from the east several times to
recover artifacts from the outer approach — objects left by people who turned back,
or by travelers who didn't. She has not been deep into them.

She knows the terrain is disorienting — the stone formations produce visual sameness.
She knows the wail is not supernatural (wind through fractured rock) but that the
sustained tone affects judgment in ways that are hard to describe. She knows that
people who stay too long in the Badlands come back quieter than they left, if they
come back at all.

She goes in anyway because Jean is going in, and because whatever is in there that
interests him now interests her.

---

## Future Role (Beyond the Transition)

Mara's stated guide service ends at the Echoing Caves. What happens after is open.
She could:
- Continue into the Badlands with Jean (drawn in by curiosity and something in Jean
  she hasn't finished reading)
- Part ways at the caves and reappear later on the Resolute Plains (Chapter 4),
  where her trading routes take her
- Become a recurring presence — someone Jean encounters at intervals across the
  second half of the game, each time knowing him slightly better than before

Her recurring thread, if used: she is building a picture of Jean from fragments,
the way she builds a picture of an artifact. By the end of the game, she may
have assembled more of Jean's true history than he has told anyone.

---

## What to Avoid

- **Do not make her a mentor figure.** She has observations; she offers them sparingly
  and practically, not as wisdom. She is not Votha Krr.
- **Do not make her warmly maternal.** Her care for Jean, if it develops, is expressed
  as attention and competence, not comfort.
- **Do not explain her.** Her backstory, her reasons for nomadic life, her history
  with Devet — these are implied rather than delivered. She does not tell Jean her story.
- **Do not let her ask Jean direct questions about himself.** She considers extraction
  rude. She waits. If something is offered, she receives it. She does not pull.
- **Do not have her react to Gorran with fear or excessive wonder.** She is interested,
  not overwhelmed. She has catalogued stranger things.
