# Corren — Character Profile

## Overview

Corren was a scavenger and route-finder who worked the western approaches,
cave systems, and near-edges of the Wailing Badlands for most of his adult
life. He is not present in the game at the time of Jean's arrival in Aurelion —
he disappeared into the Wailing Badlands two years before Jean's journey begins.
He is Mara's former mentor and the closest thing she has had to a father figure
since her own father's death.

He matters in the story not as a character the player meets but as an absence
that shaped Mara profoundly — and as a thread that may surface in Chapter 3,
deep in the Badlands, in the form of evidence he left behind.

---

## Who He Was

Corren was in his mid-fifties when Mara knew him. He was weathered in the way
that comes from decades outdoors and had the kind of stillness that isn't
passivity but economy — he didn't move unless it was worth moving, didn't speak
unless it was worth saying. He had the same dry humor as Mara, which is either
where she learned it or why they got along, or both.

He was meticulous in his work: every find catalogued, every route annotated,
every campsite left as he found it. He kept his gear in a specific order that he
never varied. He could read terrain the way other people read text — not just
"what is here" but "what has been here and how long ago and which direction."

He had no family that Mara knew about. He did not talk about his past before
scavenging. He answered direct questions about himself with something adjacent
to the answer and then moved on. Mara, who considers extraction rude and waits
for things to be shown, found this entirely compatible. They understood each
other without explanation.

He took her on because she was good at the work and because she didn't ask him
to be something he wasn't. He taught her the western approaches, the cave
systems, the near-Badlands terrain, the specific knowledge of how far you could
go into the outer approaches before the disorientation became a problem. He
taught her to read his systems, which she then modified into her own.

---

## The Disappearance

Two years ago Corren told Mara he had a lead. He wouldn't say on what. This
was unusual — he was not secretive about his work as a rule, and he had shared
leads with her before. Whatever this was, he had decided she shouldn't come.
He didn't explain why, and she didn't ask. She had learned that he would tell
her things when he had decided to tell her.

He went into the Wailing Badlands alone.

He did not come back.

She waited a reasonable amount of time and then went to the threshold to look
for sign of him. She found his pack.

It was set down carefully, off the main approach, weighted with a flat stone
so it wouldn't move if the wind came up. Everything inside was organized in
his exact system. His navigation tools were there. His food was there. His
annotated route maps were there — including, she discovered, a set of maps
for the interior of the Badlands she hadn't known he had. He had been in
further than he'd told her.

There was no note. There was no body. There was nothing to indicate whether
he had gone in without his pack deliberately — as someone who had decided
they weren't coming back and wanted their things found — or whether he had
simply set it down at a specific waypoint and something had happened before
he could return for it.

She has not resolved this. She does not try to.

She took the pack. She kept the maps. She has not told anyone about any of this.
She refers to Corren in the past tense when his name comes up, without elaboration.
She has not gone into the Badlands since.

Until now.

---

## In Chapter 3 — The Corren Thread

Corren's maps, which Mara carries but has not shown Jean, cover the outer and
middle Badlands in significant detail. The inner Badlands — the deep territory —
have annotations that grow sparser and then stop at a specific point. The last
annotation is a coordinate reference to something he has labeled only as
*"the still place."*

As Jean and Mara move into the Badlands, Mara will be navigating from these
maps. She will not acknowledge this immediately. If Jean notices she is reading
from something, she will answer directly: they were made by someone she knew.
She will not say more unless asked further.

Evidence of Corren in the Badlands is optional and should be deployed sparingly:

- **A marked camp** — a fire ring in an unusual location, organized the way he
  organized everything, now cold and beginning to weather. Nothing missing from
  it. Nothing disturbed. As if he stepped away and didn't come back.
- **A cairn** — in a place with no obvious reason for one, stacked with the
  precision of someone who builds things to last. No inscription. No explanation.
- **The still place** — whatever Corren's final annotation pointed to. This should
  not be resolved in a single beat. It is a location that warrants its own
  design pass when Chapter 3's deep Badlands territory is built.

What the Corren thread gives Mara: a reason to be here that she doesn't have
to explain to Jean, a grief that runs parallel to his without being the same
shape, and the possibility that whatever Corren found — or chose — in the
Badlands is something she is about to find as well.

---

## Narrative Notes

- Corren should never be explained. What he was looking for, why he went alone,
  why he left the pack — these are questions the story can hold open. Some of
  them may resolve in Chapter 3. Some should not.
- Do not write Corren as tragic. He was deliberate in everything he did. If he
  went into the Badlands without his pack, it was a choice, not a mistake.
- Do not make his disappearance supernatural in a cheap way. The Badlands affect
  judgment and orientation; what happens to people who go deep is ambiguous by
  design. Corren's fate should honor that ambiguity.
- He matters to Mara in a way she has not processed. This thread is hers, not
  Jean's — though Jean, who understands what it is to carry someone you can't
  name, may recognize the shape of it without being told.
