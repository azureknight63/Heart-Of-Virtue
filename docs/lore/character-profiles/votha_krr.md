# Elder Votha Krr

## The Unhurried Guide
Votha Krr is an ancient Golemite and a leading member of the council of Grondia. Towering and austere, his presence is defined by a deep-set patience—"patient, unhurried, the way a canyon regards a river." When he speaks, his voice carries a quiet weight, devoid of urgency, conveying a sense that the events he speaks of have already been settled by time.

He serves as Jean's first true guide in Grondia, offering him both practical direction and the beginning of a deeper spiritual inquiry.

## Physical Appearance
In many ways, Votha Krr shares the massive, imposing Golemite physiology seen in Gorran—built of heavy, earth-hewn stone with a towering frame. However, his ancient age is immediately apparent:
* **Weathered Stone Hide:** Unlike Gorran's relatively smooth, solid form, Votha Krr's stone skin is deeply weathered, showing the pitting, erosion, and deep fissures of a being who has lived through millennia. Fissures and small cracks web across his chest and arms, giving him the look of an ancient cliffside.
* **Ancient Overgrowth:** He is covered in far more moss than Gorran. Thick, carpet-like patches of ancient, luminous green-and-gold moss drape over his shoulders, back, and the crown of his head like ivy climbing old ruins. 
* **The Living Metaphor:** This heavy moss growth acts as a physical representation of his philosophy; vibrant, soft life thrives directly inside the deep cracks and weathered hollows of his stone form.

## The Council and the Citadel
Within the stone halls of the Grondia Citadel, Votha Krr represents the seasoned leadership of the Golemite people. While he holds a title of authority, he remains modest and grounded, observing dryly that "some among us are more foolish than others in how much weight they give that title." 

He is deeply responsible for the welfare of his community, recognizing the threat that the slime infestation poses to the sacred Grondelith Mineral Pools southwest of the city—a corruption lethal to Golemite kind, but one he trusts Jean's human constitution can withstand.

## Philosophy of the Cracks
Votha Krr is the source of a central theme in Jean's journey: 

> "To mend what is broken, one must first understand the cracks."

He does not offer Jean easy comfort or simple answers. When Jean deflects questions about his destination or identity, Votha accepts the evasion not with pity, but with an ancient recognition. Instead of pressing for answers, he points Jean toward the work that needs doing—both the physical cleansing of the Grondelith Pools and, eventually, the search for lost things in the Echoing Caves. 

His silent gesture—pressing two fingers briefly to his chest over where a human heart would reside—serves as a wordless reminder to Jean of the internal healing that must take place.

## The Secret of the Stones
Votha Krr possesses a deep, elemental understanding of the world's memory. When Jean returns from the pools with a mineral fragment, Votha explains its nature:

> "A memory, made stone. The mineral pools do not merely hold water—they record what passes through them. Light, creature, time. This fragment carries something very old. It is right that it returns to stone."

Votha's consumption of the fragment—a traditional Golemite act of internalizing memory—reveals his role as a keeper of the earth's history. 

## A Burden of Silence
Despite his wisdom, Votha Krr is not without his own evasiveness. When questioned about the Broken Gate in the Wailing Badlands, he becomes uncharacteristically quiet. This silence hints at a profound Golemite shame or a forbidden history tied to their ancestors' failed attempts to touch the divine—a burden of the past that he shields from outsiders, leaving Jean to discover the truth on the road.

**Pronoun convention:** Like all Golemites, Votha Krr uses **he/him/his** pronouns.
