# Amelia Claire
## Overview
- Name: Amelia Claire
- Age: would have been 30 at the time of her passing (age expressed as memory/echo)
- Occupation: Former primary school teacher in the world Jean remembers; in Aurelion she is an elusive memory and muse.
- Appearance: Soft features, dark hair often pinned back, warm hazel eyes. In Jean's recollections she wears a well-worn shawl and carries a small satchel of books and notes.
- Personality: Tender, patient, and quietly brave. Amelia embodied steadiness — a calm presence who listened more than she spoke and offered bravery through gentleness. She had a streak of practical optimism and a wry sense of humor that could ease the heaviest moments.
- Background: In Jean's life before Aurelion, Amelia was the anchor of their small family: she taught neighborhood children, kept the household running, and nurtured Jean's softer ambitions. Her life was ordinary in ways that made it extraordinary: warm breakfasts, bandaids on skinned knees, and stories read aloud by lamplight. Her death is a central wound for Jean; in Aurelion she lingers as half-remembered phrases, a scent on a coat, or a dream that fades upon waking.

## Important relationships
- Jean Claire: Amelia's husband; their marriage was marked by deep mutual respect and quiet devotion. Amelia's memory is Jean's primary tether to the life he lost.
- Regina Claire: Their daughter. Amelia was fiercely protective and endlessly patient with Regina; family moments and lullabies shaped Regina's earliest memories.
- Gorran: While Gorran is alien to Amelia's original life, in Jean's mind Gorran becomes an extension of the promise Jean made to Amelia that their family would be safe — an execution of that promise, however tangential.

## Role in the Story
Amelia functions less as an active character and more as the emotional north star of Jean's journey. Her presence haunts scenes with small, domestic details: a hand-stitched ribbon, a half-finished letter, or a song hummed in an empty room. She represents the ordinary life Jean yearns to rebuild — safety, routine, and the fragile courage of small acts. In many ways, she is Jean's St. Therese of Liseaux; full of love and devotion, taking joy in the small pleasures - and struggles - of everyday life.

Her influence drives Jean's moral choices. When faced with cruelty or easy violence, memories of Amelia's gentleness restrain him; when offered chances to flee, thoughts of the family she nurtured pull him toward accountability. Quest hooks tied to Amelia might include locating remnants of their life (a locket, a bonnet, a child's drawing), fulfilling promises once made, or uncovering why Jean's memories blurred between worlds.

## Themes and Motifs
- Home and Hearth: Amelia embodies the idea that home is made of daily, loving labor rather than grand gestures.
- Memory and Loss: She illustrates how memory preserves scent, sound, and tiny gestures — not always whole facts.
- Quiet Courage: Her bravery is shown in small, steady acts rather than battlefield glory.

## Suggested In-Game Touches
- Items: A frayed shawl that grants temporary calm; a lullaby that soothes frightened NPCs; a child's crayon drawing that unlocks a memory scene.
- Encounters: Dream sequences where Amelia's voice offers a clue or comfort
