# The Eastern Approach — Environment Profile
## Grondia's Eastern Gate to the River Crossing

This document covers the transitional geography between Grondia's eastern gate
and the river west of the foothills — the route Jean travels at the start of the
Chapter 2-3 transition before reaching the nomad camp and crossing west.

---

## Overview

The Eastern Approach is not a named region in the Golemite tradition — it is
simply the outside of the mountain, the terrain that exists between Grondia's
boundary and the open lowlands. It has no permanent inhabitants. It is a path,
not a place.

Its function in the narrative is decompression: after the confined underground
world of the mineral pools and Grondia's citadel, Jean emerges onto exposed
mountain face and descends into open terrain. The Resolute Plains are visible
to the east from the high trail. He walks the other direction.

---

## Route Description

### GateEast (Grondia tile 15,5) — The Mountain's Eastern Face

The eastern gate opens onto raw rock and cold air. The citadel's warmth
disappears immediately. Sky is visible — genuinely visible, not through a gap
or a vent, but as the full sky above exposed terrain.

The gate itself is Golemite-built: massive, functional, undecorated. It is
not the primary entrance to Grondia (that is GateSouth, currently sealed). It
is a working gate — used for monitoring the eastern approaches, for resource
scouts, for Gorran's occasional patrols. It does not feel ceremonial.

Standing at the gate looking east: the mountain face descends in a series of
rock shelves and switchbacks. The foothills begin where the stone softens.
Further east — visible from the high point of the gate — the terrain opens
into the first green distance of the Resolute Plains.

Jean sees this and walks south.

---

### The South Descent

A short rocky trail runs south along the mountain's eastern face, descending
via switchbacks. It is not maintained in the human sense — no gravel, no
retaining walls — but it is worn: enough feet, over enough time, have chosen
the same route that the path exists by accumulation rather than construction.

**Sensory profile:**
- Cold wind with no consistent direction — the mountain face deflects and
  channels it unpredictably
- The smell of cold stone; a faint mineral quality from the exposed rock
- Very little sound except wind and footfall
- Views eastward open and close as the trail bends around outcroppings

Gorran moves more slowly on the descent. He trails one hand against the cliff
face at intervals — not for balance, but the habitual attentiveness of a Golemite
to exposed stone. He is reading it. He does not explain what he reads.

**Narrative note**: The trail is short. Jean doesn't linger here. The descent
is physical transition — the body leaving Grondia before the mind does.

---

### The Westward Wind — Foothills and the River Valley

At the base of the descent, the trail flattens and begins to wind westward. The
terrain is scrubby and rough — not the open lushness of the Resolute Plains to the
east, but not the hostile geology of the Badlands to the west either. This is
transitional land: exposed, wind-worn, sparsely vegetated, not obviously claimed
by any ecology.

**Sensory profile:**
- Scrub grass and low, bent plants adapted to wind and thin soil
- Occasional exposed rock formations — not dramatic, just the mountain's
  foundations showing through
- The light is different out here: broader, less filtered
- Wind from the west carries no particular scent at first, then — faintly,
  after an hour's walk — the smell of moving water

**Gorran in open terrain:**

Golemites are not built for open ground. Gorran moves with competence but without
the ease he shows in confined stone passages. He is large, deliberate, and the
open air around him has a quality of exposure that he accepts rather than seeks.
He does not complain. He does not suggest turning back. He walks.

Jean, who has spent most of his Aurelion time in enclosed spaces, registers the
openness as something between relief and unease. He doesn't name either.

---

### The River Valley — First Sight

The trail descends gradually into a shallow valley. The sound arrives before the
sight: a consistent low rush, continuous, beneath the wind.

The river comes into view from the lip of the valley: wide, cold, moving with
purpose from north to south. The east bank is near. The west bank is across a
distance that is not dangerous but requires a crossing.

The east bank is flat and accessible — the kind of riverbank that invites
camping because it is level, sheltered from the prevailing wind by the valley
walls, and close to water. There is a camp here: the nomad company.

**The river's character:**

- Width: approximately 30 meters (varies by season)
- Current: moderate and consistent — not white water, but not gentle either
- Clarity: high; cold-water runoff from the mountain range to the north
- The ford exists at a specific point north of the camp, marked by different
  surface texture. Mara knows it.

**Physical camp description:**

- Lean-tos and covered frames that go up and come down efficiently — the
  infrastructure of people who move often and have refined the practice
- The central fire is properly built: small, hot, positioned to limit smoke
- Artifact samples are visible in organized clusters near Mara's lean-to —
  not carelessly scattered but arranged in a system
- The raft is visible at the bank, tied to a fixed rock: wide, flat, purpose-built
  for crossing, not beautiful
- Signs of previous camps in the same ring: old ash, worn ground

## Geography: What Lies in Other Directions

### East (Near) — The Mountain's Eastern Face

Just outside GateEast: the rocky descent, the mountain's exposed eastern slope.
No permanent inhabitants. Golemite scouts pass through occasionally. Most
travelers approaching Grondia come from the south or southeast, not the east.

### East (Far) — The Resolute Plains (Chapter 4 Territory)

Visible from the high trail, the Resolute Plains begin where the foothills
flatten completely. Wide rolling grasslands, caravan roads, scattered human
settlements, and the nomadic community of the Wayfarers who range its interior.
This is civilized territory by Aurelion's standards — busy, human-dominated,
connected by trade routes.

Jean does not go east. By the time he is ready for the Resolute Plains (Chapter 4,
the Bargaining stage), he will have crossed through the Badlands first. The player
who sees the plains from the high trail and then watches Jean walk the other way
understands something Jean doesn't yet.

### Southeast — The Plains Approach and the Trader Waystation

The southeastern route follows the mountain's southern skirt — a longer but
more navigable path than the eastern trail. This is how most human traders
approach Grondia: not through Verdette Caverns, but up from the plains via a
managed road.

At the junction of the plains approach and the mountain's base, there is likely a
small waystation: a permanent rest point for traders making the final climb. Human-
operated, faction-neutral. A place with news, rumors, and practical supplies.
Jean does not visit this during the transition sequence, but it exists and could
be accessed later.

Continuing southeast: the terrain gradually dries. The Resolute Plains to the east
give way to transitional scrubland further south, eventually becoming the arid
wastes that the plains profile places on its southern boundary.

### South (GateSouth, Grondia tile 10,10 — Sealed)

The southern gate of Grondia leads to the mountain's lower reaches — older
construction, less-used passages, the geological foundations of the citadel.

This area is stubbed for future content. The gate is sealed from the inside
and its description in Grondia gives no clue as to what lies beyond. What exists
there, and why the gate is sealed, is undetermined. Possible future content:
the lower Golemite reaches that predate Grondia's current form, with connections
to the schism history seeded in the Western Verdette Caverns lore rewards.

### West — River → Echoing Caves → Wailing Badlands

narrative sequence. See `docs/lore/environments/wailing-badlands/` for the
