# Cut Content: False Vision Draft (Wailing Badlands)

**Source**: `Ch01PostRumbler3` wrong-choice branches, removed in issue #134.

**Concept**: Jean experiences a false vision in the Wailing Badlands — a vivid, haunting
replay of the moment in the Verdette Caverns where he might have turned and run. The vision
shows the road not taken: Jean fleeing the rock-man, escaping the chamber, and being destroyed
by something far worse waiting outside.

The sequence could function as a nightmare, a seer's curse, or an environmental effect
(hallucinogenic spores, a corrupted shrine, the badlands' ambient wrongness). It ends the
same way regardless of any player input — it is a vision, not a choice.

---

## Draft Prose

### Beat 1 — The Flight

*Jean swallows hard and begins sprinting toward the hole in the chamber wall.*

*(alternate opening — indecision variant)*
*Unsure of what to do, Jean stands frozen, glancing between the rock-man and his avenue of
escape.*

---

### Beat 2 — The Rock-Man Falls

Rock-man manages to smash one of the beasts beneath his large column-turned-bludgeon,
however one of the other beasts jumps on his back at that moment, knocking him
quickly to the ground. The other beasts pile on and begin slashing and biting
mercilessly.

---

### Beat 3 — The Escape

Just before Jean can make his escape, a beast jumps and slides between Jean and
his salvation, kicking up loose dirt and pebbles. Jean quickly dodges to the side
and tries to circle back to the entrance from which he came.
Jumping around the snapping jaws and swinging tails of the other beasts,
he manages to make it back to the long bridge connecting the two spires.

---

### Beat 4 — False Hope

He can hear the beasts behind him, but they seemed to have stopped at
the threshold separating the chamber entrance and the outside,
as if they are afraid of the daylight.

Jean straightens up and begins to catch his breath. Just as he breathes
a sigh of relief, a piercing screech rings through his ears,
a great cold wind blows over him, and two sharp claws dig
ruthlessly into his shoulders, picking him up off of the
bridge.

---

### Beat 5 — The Abomination

He looks up at the horrible monster that grabbed him and
is terrified at the ugly abomination. Rows upon rows
of jagged teeth, three sunken black eyes staring
hungrily at him, a froth of saliva spilling out of
its disgusting maw.

Jean gasps and gropes uselessly at the sharp claws
still digging painfully into his flesh. He swings
his mace and lands a blow on the twisted, scaly
leg of the abominable demon.

The monster lets out a loud screech, and swooping
quickly, smashes Jean's body against the wall of rock.

---

### Beat 6 — The End of the Vision

Jean screams in pain, his mace arm swinging uselessly by
his side, broken.

Again, the monster smashes Jean against the wall,
repeatedly and without hesitation or mercy —
and slams him hard into the ground.

*[Vision ends. Jean wakes, gasping, wherever the sequence is triggered.]*

---

## Implementation Notes

- The vision should be non-interactive or single-continue-button only — no choices.
- Jean takes no actual damage; the sequence is purely narrative.
- The abomination is deliberately unnamed and undescribed beyond its physical horror —
  it could be tied to whatever threatens the Badlands, or left ambiguous.
- Consider pairing with a short recovery beat: Jean steadying himself, questioning what
  he saw, perhaps a line from a companion or the environment grounding him.
